Summary of Reflection:-

Imagine the internet as a giant neighborhood that has changed over time.In the beginning, during the 1990s, the internet was like a massive public library. You could walk in and read pages, but you could not write in the books, leave comments, or add your own stories. You were just a quiet visitor.Eventually, the internet turned into a global town square. Suddenly, anyone could upload videos, share photos, and chat with friends. However, there was a hidden catch: a few giant landlords built the town square, owned all the buildings, and secretly tracked everything you did to sell ads. You got to participate, but you did not own anything.Now, a new internet is being built, and it looks like a co-op housing community. Instead of one billionaire landlord owning the entire building, every resident owns a tiny piece of the brick and mortar. No single person can lock you out, change the rules, or sell your personal secrets without your permission.This new internet was impossible to build years ago because our computers were too weak and our internet connections were too slow to let everyone work together. Today, it allows us to share data, send money, and connect with people globally without needing a middleman like a bank or a massive tech corporation to watch over us. Learning about this shift helps us understand how we are finally taking back ownership of our digital lives.
