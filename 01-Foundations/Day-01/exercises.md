Why wasn't Web3 invented in the 1990s?-

Web3 could not be invented in the 1990s because the fundamental cryptographic math, decentralized computing power, and high-speed internet infrastructure did not exist yet. Building Web3 requires a highly advanced stack of technology; trying to run a modern blockchain in 1995 would be like trying to run a smartphone app on a 1980s graphing calculator.

Why can't blockchain exist without networking?-

🌐 Why Blockchain Needs NetworkingA blockchain cannot exist without networking because it relies entirely on a distributed peer-to-peer (P2P) network to synchronize data, achieve consensus, and maintain security. Without a network connection, individual computers (nodes) would be isolated silos unable to broadcast new transactions, validate blocks, or agree on a single, shared version of the ledger, completely destroying the system's tamper-proof and decentralized nature.

If Facebook became decentralized tomorrow, what might improve? What new challenges might appear?-

👥 A Decentralized Facebook: Benefits and ChallengesIf Facebook became decentralized, user privacy and data ownership would improve drastically, as a single corporation could no longer monetize or weaponize personal data, and users would gain censorship-resistant control over their own content. However, this shift would introduce severe challenges, such as the inability to effectively moderate hate speech, illegal content, or misinformation, alongside sluggish platform performance and a significantly more complex, less user-friendly login and recovery experience.

🎯 When Centralization is BetterA centralized solution remains the superior 
choice in emergency response services, such as a city's emergency dispatch or disaster management command, where split-second decision-making, strict hierarchy, and immediate top-down coordination are vital to saving lives, making the slow voting and consensus delays of a decentralized system dangerous.
